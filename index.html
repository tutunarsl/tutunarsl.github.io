Biography

I graduated from my master’s degree Robotics, Systems and Control at ETH Zurich, Switzerland. Before that, I received my bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering with first place in my cohort and I recived my second bachelor degree in Control Engineering and Automotion.

My research interests mainly focus on incorporating cutting-edge machine learning approaches into perception and mapping. Specifically, I specialize in robust localization and perception in degenerate and challenging environments. Besides, I am highly interested in long-term map maintanence and fast volumetric mapping.

For more information, please refer to my latest Curriculum Vitae.

-, 2022 (In-progress) to be submitted to IEEE Transactions on Robotics (T-RO)
LA-ICP: Localizability-Aware Scene Analysis and Constrained Optimization for Robust Localization

In this work, we prose a full localizability framework i) to detect (non-)localizability of the environment along 6 degrees of freedom, and ii) to use this information in the underlying optimization in scenarios of low localizability, allowing for a better convergence of the ICP algorithm. Our method uses geometric relations between the environment and the principal directions of the optimization as part of its multicategory LiDAR localizability analysis. This localizability categorization is then tightly integrated into the scan-to-map point-cloud registration process to allow pose updates only along well- constrained directions.
Philip Arm, Gabriel Waibel, Jan Preisig, Turcan Tuna, Ruyi Zhou, Valentin Bickel, Gabriela Ligeza, Takahiro Miki, Floarian Kehl, Hendrik MiKolvenbach, Marco Hutter,
-, 2022 Under-review in Journal of Science Robotics
Scientific Exploration of Challenging Planetary Analog Environments with a Team of Legged Robots

This article presents a team of legged robots for exploration missions in challenging planetary analog environments. We equipped the robots with an efficient locomotion controller, a mapping pipeline for online and post-mission visualization, semantic and instance segmentation to highlight scientific targets, and advanced payloads for remote and in-situ scientific investigation.

Deep learning frameworks to learn prediction and simulation focused control system models

In this work, we present Simulation and prediction focuced DNNs for non-linear system identification problem.
