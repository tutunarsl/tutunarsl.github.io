Biography

I am currently a master’s student in Robotics, Systems and Control at ETH Zurich, Switzerland. Before that, I received my bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering with Excellent Graduate Honor as a two-time National Scholarship winner from Tongji University, Shanghai, China.

Motivated by the interdisciplinary nature of artificial intelligence, I have indulged in diverse robotic fields through my research exploration. Since April 2022, I have been engaged as a research intern at the Autonomous Learning Group at Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems (MPI-IS), Tübingen, Germany, focusing on developing agile skills for legged systems through generative adversarial imitation of rough demonstrations under the supervision of Dr. Georg Martius.

My knowledge of legged robotic control using reinforcement learning techniques was developed and refined during my master’s study, especially during my work on skill integration for ANYmal with hierarchical reinforcement learning supervised by Prof. Marco Hutter at Robotic Systems Lab (RSL), ETH Zurich, Switzerland. Prior to that, I was also honored to work on the design of a passive tail with distributional stiffness for Pleurobot supervised by Prof. Auke Ijspeert at Biorobotics Laboratory (BioRob), EPFL, Switzerland.

My research interests mainly focus on incorporating cutting-edge machine learning approaches into robotic control, especially for developing robust skills for legged systems using reinforcement learning techniques.

For more information, please refer to my latest Curriculum Vitae.

By employing a novel compositional control structure, ANYmal learned to achieve full-pose trajectory tracking in a global coordinate system. A hierarchical system pipeline was introduced, where the high-level policy proceeds global target information and outputs low-level composition signals and commands with trivial regularization.
